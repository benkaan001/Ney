Incredible History

Ney is known as the instrument of patience. Once harvested, Ney goes through a very meticulous period of processing that lasts nearly two whole years. Though it may take months for a Ney player to get the most basic sound out of it, it is believed to take a life time to learn to play it. It is also believed that humans don't live long enough to truly master the instrument.

Facts about Ney

Ney is believed to originate from around 5,000 B.C. The oldest Ney available today belongs to the ancient Sumerians and is being exhibited in the Babylonian section at the Penn Museum in Philadelphia.

Facts about Ney

Due to its mystic sound qualities, Ney has been an instrument played in rituals and celebrations throughout the history of mankind. "Mesmerizing", "mysterious", "harmonic" are among the worlds that historians have translated from ancient records that refer to Ney.

Facts about Ney

Ney was born in the Mesopotamia, aka "cradle of civilizations". Therefore, it has been associated with numerous religions and belief systems throughout the history.
